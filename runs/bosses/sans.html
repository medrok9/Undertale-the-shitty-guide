Sans Boss Guide

Overview

Sans is the final and most difficult fight in the Genocide Route. Unlike every other enemy, he ignores standard combat rules: he dodges every attack, applies KR (Karmic Retribution) damage, and forces the player into a long endurance test rather than a traditional fight.

Key rule: You do not win by damage output. You win by surviving everything.

Core Mechanics Explained

KR (Karmic Retribution)

KR is a poison-like effect that reduces HP over time after taking damage. Even after dodging successfully later, KR can still kill you if ignored. This forces careful healing timing instead of panic healing.

Dodging System

Sans does not follow normal attack telegraphs. Instead:

  • He mixes bones + blasters in unpredictable sequences
  • He uses timing traps (delayed hits)
  • He punishes movement spam

Blue / Orange Attacks

  • Blue bones: you must NOT move or you take heavy damage
  • Orange bones: you MUST move through them quickly

Phase Breakdown (Important)

Phase 1 — Opening Pressure

Sans starts with bone waves and simple Gaster Blaster positioning. This phase is designed to drain healing items early.

  • Short bone bursts (horizontal + vertical)
  • Basic blaster sweeps
  • Platform forcing (left/right movement traps)

Phase 2 — Rhythm Disruption

Attacks become intentionally inconsistent. Timing is no longer stable. This is where most players start losing control of movement rhythm.

  • Mixed bone + blaster combos
  • Sudden direction changes
  • Short reaction windows (less than 1 second)

Phase 3 — Platform Control

Sans manipulates gravity-style movement and forces tight jumps.

  • Platform shifting attacks
  • Corner trapping
  • Delayed bone drops

Phase 4 — Final Attack

The longest and most punishing sequence in the fight. You are locked into survival with minimal safe zones.

  • Extended bone gauntlet
  • Continuous blaster tracking
  • Minimal healing windows

Survival Strategy (High-Level Play)

  1. Do NOT panic move: Most deaths come from over-correction.
  2. Memorize sequences: The fight is pattern-based, not random.
  3. Heal only in safe windows: Usually after blaster cycles.
  4. Small movement only: Large dodges get you hit into secondary attacks.
  5. Stay mentally steady: The fight is designed to cause fatigue errors.

Final Sequence

After exhausting his full attack rotation, Sans collapses. At this point, he will stop dodging temporarily, allowing the final hit to land.

"You're gonna have a bad time."
